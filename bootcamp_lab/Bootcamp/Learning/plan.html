Upskilling Program Plan

AI Engineering and Applied ML Development Program

A structured learning and capstone plan designed as a serious development rotation with strong mentorship, evaluation, and hiring integration.

Time Commitment & Allocation

Expected Time Commitment: 20 hours per week
Program Duration: approximately 13–14 weeks
Total Effort: approximately 210+ hours
Phase Duration Weekly Commitment Total Hours
Structured Learning 4 weeks 20 hrs/week 80 hrs
Capstone Development 6 weeks 20 hrs/week 120 hrs
Reviews & Interviews ~1–2 weeks Variable ~10–15 hrs

This program should be treated as a structured development rotation with meaningful manager support and protected capacity.

Program Structure

Phase 1: Structured Technical Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

Focused, instructor-led sessions with hands-on implementation.

Week 1 – ML & Neural Foundations

  • Python data science basics: NumPy, pandas
  • Regression and classification
  • Metrics, train/test splits, overfitting and underfitting
  • Perceptrons, backpropagation, and losses
  • PyTorch training loops
  • Regularization fundamentals
  • Transformer foundations: tokenization, embeddings, attention

Week 2 – LLM Fine-Tuning & RAG

  • Dataset collection, cleaning, and formatting
  • Continued pretraining vs. SFT
  • PEFT: LoRA/QLoRA
  • Error analysis loops
  • Semantic search and vector DB basics
  • Context injection and hallucination mitigation

Week 3 – Prompting & Evaluation

  • Zero-shot vs. few-shot prompting
  • Reasoning-style prompting
  • Iteration frameworks
  • Evaluation frameworks and benchmarking
  • A/B and shadow testing
  • Reward modeling overview
  • RLHF overview and trade-offs

Week 4 – Reinforcement Learning

  • RLHF concepts and trade-offs vs. supervised fine-tuning
  • Preference data collection and reward design principles
  • Common RL instability issues and failure modes
  • Designing automated graders using LLM-as-a-judge and rubric-based systems
  • Calibration, validation, and bias mitigation in grading models
  • Using graders to power iterative training and optimization loops
  • Offline vs. online RL and safe deployment strategies
  • Telemetry-driven reward iteration and continuous improvement
  • Determining when RL is appropriate vs. improving data, architecture, or evaluation design

Phase 2: Capstone (Weeks 5–12)

Each participant must complete a business-relevant AI capstone project.

  • Define a business-relevant AI problem
  • Design an appropriate architecture: fine-tuning vs. RAG vs. hybrid
  • Implement an evaluation framework
  • Conduct structured error analysis
  • Iterate based on measurable metrics
  • Document trade-offs and limitations
  • Provide a production-readiness plan
Mentorship Model
  • Assigned paired mentor
  • Biweekly deep-dive reviews
  • Architecture reviews
  • Evaluation critique
  • Readiness assessment prior to hiring committee review

Phase 3: Capstone Review & Interview Process

This program is tightly integrated with a Dedicated Hiring Committee.

Step 1 – Capstone Review

  • Conducted by the Hiring Committee
  • Formal technical presentation
  • Architecture defense
  • Evaluation rigor review
  • Responsible AI considerations
  • Scalability and production trade-offs

Only candidates who pass capstone review move forward.

Phase 4: Technical Interviews

All interviews are conducted by members of the Hiring Committee.

  • Round 1 – Breadth
  • Round 2 – Depth

Final Step – Hiring Committee Vote

  • Coordinator submits final candidate package
  • Hiring Committee reviews capstone evaluation, interview performance, mentor feedback, and technical artifacts
  • Majority vote determines whether the candidate can proceed to role update
