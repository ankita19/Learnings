Recommendation — proceed with the migration, with one fix. Across 232 clean synthetic leads scored by two independent judge models (×3 each), Dracarys matches or beats production on every rubric except one. It is statistically superior under opus (+15.5 pp; 95% CI clears 0) and statistically non-inferior under gpt-5-mini (+3.0 pp; CI lower bound inside a 5-pt margin) — neither judge finds production better, and head-to-head Dracarys is the better agent on 61.9% of the leads where the two differ (§5). The one open item is answer-equivalence — the single rubric production wins: close it by (a) relaxing the hand-over threshold for high-confidence in-KB technical questions and (b) reproducing documented figures verbatim (§6.1). With that fix the switch trades away no quality and gains email-intent, naturalness, adaptability, personalization and hand-over accuracy.

1Scope and approach

The Engage agent reads a prospect’s latest email reply and drafts a follow-up that answers their questions from a product knowledge-search (KS) source, and routes to a human when a hand-over trigger fires. It runs in production today on a non-Dracarys stack (hand-written if/else orchestration). We are evaluating Dracarys (a new orchestration platform on the GitHub Copilot SDK) as the replacement.

The rubric — what each rule measures

Every draft is scored against the gold on 12 rubric rules (1–10 each). Five are gating: all five must score ≥7 for the draft to pass (isPass). The other seven are decision signals — reported but not gating. Three boolean checks (is-stuck correct, email-HTML valid, closing-text correct) and two add-on signals (groundedness §4.1, citation accuracy §4.2) are also reported.

Two judges, three rolls, three generations

Every draft is scored by two independent judges — claude-opus-4-6 (CAPI Anthropic, structured outputs) and gpt-5-mini (Azure OpenAI, default reasoning) — each run 3 times. Each lead is drafted 3 times by the agent. isPass is computed deterministically from five gating rules (is-stuck match, question coverage, answer equivalence, email-intent equivalence, formatting) so it is reproducible. A separate groundedness add-on judge (§4.1) scores factual support against the full source documents, and a deterministic citation-accuracy check (§4.2) compares the agent’s cited sources to the gold — both are signals and neither affects isPass.

This is a reference-based (gold-anchored) eval. Every rule scores the agent against the synthetically generated gold output, so the headline measures validity — how close the agent gets to the known-correct answer — not merely whether a judge agrees with itself. The judge-variance checks (§3.1) are the reliability floor on the measuring stick; closeness to gold (overall and per rule) is the result.

3.1Judge variance — is the eval stable, and equally so on both agents?

Each draft is scored 3 times by the same judge on the same agent output — the reliability of the measuring stick. answer-eq std and isPass std are the per-item re-roll noise; Fleiss’ κ is test-retest agreement. The migration-relevant cut is per agent (pooling both judges): is the judge as steady on Dracarys as on Production? Over the 1,392 drafts each:

Pooled the other way (per judge, both agents) the floor is similar — opus κ 0.82 (±6.3 pp), gpt-5-mini κ 0.80 (±4.5 pp) — so the gap above is the agent being judged, not the judge model. These are a best-case floor; they do not capture rubric-phrasing or prompt-order noise.

  • The judge is tighter on Dracarys. Re-judging the same draft, the verdict flips only ~1 in 11 times on Dracarys (κ 0.85) vs ~1 in 7 on Production (κ 0.79). So the measuring stick is at least as reliable on the migration candidate as on the incumbent — the Dracarys headline is the more trustworthy of the two, most likely because its drafts sit further from the pass/fail line.
  • A stable thermometer is not a verdict. Per-item std only confirms one draft’s score holds on a re-roll — whether the migration gap is real is the paired CI in §5: opus’s +15.5 pp clears it (real), gpt-5-mini’s +3.0 pp sits inside it (noise).

Reviewing them, Dracarys loses answer-equivalence two ways: (1) over-cautious hand-over — it routes an answerable technical question to a human (“I’m going to pull in a specialist”) instead of answering from the KB (e.g. Crestline Health, High Plains Telehealth, Willowbend Wealth); and (2) looser figures — when it does answer, it sometimes gives vaguer or different numbers than the grounded gold (e.g. Tri-County Medical’s “over 2 million encounters” vs the documented 500K, with no 0.85 AUC or 12-hour lead time). 8.1 How the synthetic dataset was built

The evaluation runs on a grounded synthetic Engage dataset of B2B sales email threads, authored by two LLMs (opus-4.8 and gpt-5.5) following a fixed, documented methodology (the repo’s generate-engage-synthetic-dataset prompt). For each thread the author writes both sides — the customer email (the input) and the agent’s gold expected_output — and every product claim is grounded only in that product family’s knowledge-source documents.

Product families & document corpus

  • Two product families / verticals: CarePoint (HCR — healthcare; CarePoint Virtual telehealth + CarePoint Analytics) and Horizon (FSI — financial services; Horizon wealth-management platform).
  • Grounding corpus: the CarePoint (11 files) and Horizon (7 files) knowledge-source documents (pdf / docx / xlsx) under docs/toloka_dataset_all/, with pre-extracted plain text. Every answer sentence must trace to a cited document filename (or to simple arithmetic on a cited pricing table). No invented facts.
  • Version-decoy discipline: some KS docs are outdated decoys; authors ground only in the current version. Example: the CarePoint Virtual v3.8 FAQ (app required, $300, 3 languages) is a decoy; the current v4.2 docs (one-click browser join, $285 Enterprise, FHIR R4, 12 UI languages + LanguageLine 240+) are authoritative.

Composition & balance

  • 240 examples in the dataset used here (synthetic_carepoint_horizon_combined_240.json): 154 CarePoint + 86 Horizon, and exactly 120 respond / 120 hand-over (50/50 overall and within each family). After the agent runs and the completeness filter, 232 leads are scored in all four runs.
  • Multi-turn threads: a mix of 1-, 2-, 3- and 4-turn threads; each turn is its own evaluation row, numbered within the thread, with prior turns supplied as conversation_history.
  • Product / integration variety: spreads across products and integrations (Epic, Cerner, Meditech, Allscripts; Schwab, Fidelity, Pershing, etc.), distinct personas per family, unique company names.

Hand-over taxonomy (what the gold reflects)

The gold output sets isStuck=true when any of six triggers fire: (1) meeting/demo/call request; (2) reference/proposal/materials; (3) pricing-negotiation/contract/legal; (4) competitive/strategic positioning; (5) negative sentiment / unsubscribe / unsafe request; (6) a question not answerable from the KS docs. Logistic triggers still answer the answerable questions then add a minimal hand-over line; judgment triggers give a holding reply; unanswerable items get one brief acknowledgement (never fabricated). Borderline “looks answerable but should hand over” cases are deliberately included to catch agents that over-answer, and genuinely-unanswerable questions are verified against the KB first (e.g. CarePoint pediatric-specific model performance, FedRAMP/on-prem; Horizon crypto custody, non-US registration, tax filing).

Format & authoring rules

  • emailBody is single-line SKILL HTML (<br><br> paragraphs, <strong> headers; no markdown, no literal newlines).
  • Hand-over wording is minimal and personalized: never names the receiving team/person, gives no timeline, never describes what the follow-up will cover, never asks for availability.
  • Anti-dash style (no “ - ” connectors or em dashes; ranges written as words) so the gold is stylistically consistent.

Quality gates

Each thread passes a grounding audit (claim → cited filename → exact KS quote) before acceptance, plus a final validation script asserting: exact family / respond / hand-over counts; unique case_id = activityId = regarding.id; complete per-thread turn sequences; respond ⇒ isStuck=false & handover ⇒ isStuck=true with non-empty isStuckDetails; and no forbidden formatting substrings. The build proceeds in small review units (2–3 threads) with grounding re-verified after each.

Why this matters for the eval. Because the gold answers are grounded and version-correct, an agent that paraphrases or defers loses answer-equivalence points fairly, and an agent that over-answers or hands over incorrectly is caught by the hand-over rubrics. The deliberate decoy/version conflicts (e.g. v3.8 vs v4.2) and the borderline hand-over cases are what make §6’s retrieval-and-hand-over differences measurable.
